v3.0.3 — Collages grow up.

Divider lines you can style and dial up, a proper home for finding, organizing and re-filing your projects, a clear sign of what's saved and what's already in your library, a Recycle Bin so a delete is never final, exports that match the editor pixel-for-pixel, and designer-grade snapping and guides — all still on your own hardware, with nothing uploaded.

Dividers:

Drop a crisp horizontal or vertical line into a collage, then style it like anything else: color, thickness, rounded or square ends, transparency, and an effect (outline, shadow, neon or 3D) with its own color and an adjustable strength that stays bold even on a thin line.

A real home for your collages:

The Collages start screen is now a proper project browser. Search by name, filter by category, sort by name or date, and switch between a gallery grid and a compact list. Pin your favorites to the top, jump straight back to the ones you opened most recently, and browse the rest in collapsible month and year sections — with the header and menus frozen in place so they stay put as you scroll. Re-file projects just by dragging them onto a category — or tick several and move (or delete) them together — and anything you haven't named yet simply lives under Uncategorized. The Templates tab gets the same polish, with its own search and the built-in starter layouts pinned up top.

Delete without the worry:

Remove a collage, carousel or template and it slips into your Recycle Bin rather than vanishing, with deleted templates given their own row at the top so a treasured layout is easy to spot. Restore anything with a click, or clear it for good when you're certain — and even then it goes to Windows' Recycle Bin as a last safety net.

See what's already in your library:

A green "Saved" tag now marks every collage you've exported to your photo library, with a one-tap filter to show just those — so you can tell at a glance which project you were working on.

What you see is what you save:

A collage or carousel now exports through the very same engine that draws the editor, so every color, effect, glow, logo and full-bleed background photo comes out exactly as it looked on screen.

Snap, align and measure like a designer:

Equal-spacing chains, edge-priority snapping, a green "0" on every flush edge, pull-out guides, a safe-zone for social posts, and a rules panel to switch each helper on or off. Hold Ctrl+E to glide a corner exactly into place.

Always know where it's saved:

A small note by the title stays quiet until you actually change something, then reads "Saving…" and settles to "Uncategorized — Saved" (or whichever album it's in) — so you know it's safe and exactly where it landed.

Faster and steadier:

Tracked down and fixed the periodic pauses, so arranging a busy collage stays smooth even with a large library working in the background.

Polish:

While editing text, drag across it to highlight instead of moving the box; "Check for Updates" now checks the real thing; American spelling throughout; plus dozens of smaller fixes.
