Understanding Your Results

After you run Fix, PDR shows you exactly what happened.

Confidence labels

Confirmed — date taken from authoritative metadata

Recovered — date reconstructed from reliable filename patterns

Marked — no usable date found; safe fallback rules applied

Nothing is hidden.
Nothing is silently guessed.

Use Reports History to review or export what happened at any time.

Common Questions (FAQ)

Will this overwrite or damage my original files?
No. Originals are never modified.
All changes are written to a new destination you choose.

Why are some files marked Marked?
Because no reliable date survived export or transfer.
PDR labels this clearly instead of pretending certainty.

Why don’t all files have the same confidence level?
Different apps and devices preserve metadata differently.
PDR reflects reality instead of smoothing it over.

What happens if I run Fix more than once?
Each run creates its own output and report.
Nothing is merged or overwritten automatically.

Can I stop a Fix once it starts?
Yes. Partial output remains safe and usable.
Completed work is still recorded in Reports History.

Why don’t reports change when I add more files later?
Reports are snapshots of a specific Fix run.
This preserves traceability and avoids confusion.

Why don’t I see every duplicate listed?
Exact duplicates are removed from output, not deleted.
PDR keeps the best version and explains the method used.

Can I use PDR on very large libraries?
Yes — it’s designed for scale.
Reports and UI remain usable even with large runs.

Is this safe to use with cloud backups?
Yes — if you follow the Guides.
Cloud services often strip metadata, and the Guides explain how to avoid issues.

Why does PDR feel stricter than other tools?
Because it’s deterministic and auditable.
Everything can be reviewed later.

What if something doesn’t look right?
