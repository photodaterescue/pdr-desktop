v3.0.0 — The big one: Collages & Carousels, Screen Capture, Family Trees, one-tap Sharing, and Ask PDR.

Photo Date Rescue grows from a date-repair tool into a full home for your photo library — a place to create, organize, restore and share your memories, all on your own machine with nothing uploaded. This release also folds in everything that had been lined up as v2.1 (which never shipped on its own): video transcription, photo enhancement, Needs Dates, a Recycle Bin, a working Photo Format converter, and a deep refresh of Search & Discovery and Memories.

Collages & Carousels:

A full collage studio in its own window. Start from around ten ready-made layouts, drop in your photos, then style them with frames, curved corners, captions, text and fonts, and effects (glow, shadow, blend, vignette, grain). Marquee and Ctrl-click multi-select with Canva-style equidistant snapping and measurement guides; Magic Resize keeps your layout intact when you change the canvas size. Every collage saves as a re-openable project you can come back to, and multi-page Carousels let you build an Instagram-style sequence that exports as numbered slides.

Screen Capture:

Take a screenshot or record your screen straight into your library. Blur sensitive areas live — as many as you need, with a confirmation overlay — mark zoom moments, drop in a webcam bubble, and add a microphone voiceover. Recordings save as MP4, dated the moment they were made.

Family Trees:

Turn the people you've named in People Manager into a visual family tree. Focus any person to re-center the tree, expand or collapse the bloodline and extended-family branches one at a time, and explore generation after generation while everyone stays sitting neatly beneath their own parents.

Sharing, the last mile:

Get photos out the moment you need them — drag several files out at once (with a count badge), Send to Phone over Wi-Fi by scanning a QR code, Print or Print to PDF, or Copy an image straight to the clipboard.

Ask PDR:

A built-in, fully offline helper. Ask a question in your own words ("where do my collages go?", "what does _MK mean?", "are my source photos safe?") and PDR answers straight from its own built-in help — no internet, nothing uploaded. Reach it from the sparkle button in the title bar, press Ctrl + /, or open it from Help & Support.

Photo enhancement:

The Viewer gains an Enhance panel — brightness, contrast, saturation, temperature and more, with double-click reset — plus optional on-device AI tools you opt into: face restoration (CodeFormer), 4x upscaling (Real-ESRGAN), and subject cut-out. Everything runs on your own machine; save your changes to a new file and the original stays untouched.

Video transcription:

Right-click any video and choose Transcribe. It runs a speech-to-text model on your own machine — no API key, no upload — and the Viewer overlays the spoken words. A lavender "T" badge marks transcribed videos.

Needs Dates:

A dedicated place to review the photos PDR couldn't date automatically. Pull up everything that still needs a date, set them by hand, and PDR writes the date through like any other Fix.

Recycle Bin:

Deleted files now go to a recoverable PDR Recycle Bin instead of straight to disk. Restore them, permanently delete a selection, or empty the bin — with an option to skip the Windows Recycle Bin so you actually reclaim the disk space.

Photo Format converter:

Convert your output to JPG or PNG, with EXIF dates embedded directly into the encode. The PNG path runs in a fast persistent worker, and an old infinite-loop hang on near-duplicate files that shared a date is fixed.

Search & Discovery and Memories:

The toolbar view controls and selection banners now behave as one family across Search & Discovery, Memories — Dates, Memories — Albums and Needs Dates. Send a selection from Memories to Search & Discovery and jump straight back, click a tile to select it, build albums or Parallel Libraries from a selection, and enjoy sticky day headers, month pickers, and a smoother, faster grid throughout.

Faster and steadier:

Adding a source, analysis, catalog and report generation, and the end of every Fix now run off the main process, so the window no longer locks up and the "Not Responding" flash is gone. Fix progress is driven by bytes copied with an accurate ETA, and a stuck-job notice appears if a slow drive stalls a copy so you always know PDR is still working.

Your library, your terms:

Everything stays on your own hardware — private, secure, and yours. Try it free with gentle caps, then choose how to pay: a Monthly or Yearly plan, or a one-off Lifetime license. Either way your library and photos stay yours, even if you stop paying.
