v3.0.2 — The Collage studio and Screen Capture both grow up.

A focused polish release. Collages become a genuine design tool — a professional-grade text studio, emojis with real effects, a format painter, and carousels that behave — while Screen Capture gains virtual camera backgrounds, a second camera, a full-screen camera mode, and smaller recordings. Windows open and move the instant they appear, and it's all still on your own hardware, with nothing uploaded.

A proper text studio:

Every text box now has a floating toolbar over it, mirrored in the side panel: font, the full weight range from Thin to Black, size, bold / italic / underline, alignment, UPPER / lower / Title case, effects, line spacing, transparency and color. Style individual words within a box — a different color, font, size, weight or effect on each — and change any of them mid-sentence, so only what you type next takes the new look. Choose a fixed text area and the type wraps and auto-shrinks to fit, or let it flow freely; grab a box by the text itself to move it, even while it's highlighted.

Symbols & arrows:

A new picker under Add text drops in arrows of every shape, plus stars, shapes, ticks, math and currency symbols — as ordinary text, so they take your color, font, size and effects. A gold outlined arrow or a pink neon star in two clicks.

Emojis with real effects:

Emojis are now first-class layers. Apply Looks, Effects, Glow & Shadow, Tone & Color and Flip & Rotate exactly as you would to a photo, with glow and outline that hug the emoji's actual shape.

Format painter:

Style one text box, photo or emoji just how you like it, then copy that whole look onto others — pick up the paint roller and brush it over the layers you want, or drag a box across a group to style them all in one go. Paint the whole box or just the words you tap; and when the box you pick up mixes several looks, PDR shows them and asks which one to copy. Your own colors now save into numbered slots too, alongside a dedicated PDR palette of the app's brand colors.

Carousels that behave:

Duplicate a caption or photo to the exact same spot on every page in one step; equal-margin guides and snapping now work on every page, not just the ends; set your default page count; and add stylable page outlines.

Virtual camera backgrounds:

No greenscreen needed. Drop your webcam bubble into a screen recording, open its Backdrop button, and a roomy picker opens: blur your real room or give it a softer, portrait-style depth of field, pixelate it for privacy (each with a strength slider), choose one of sixteen tasteful room scenes, or pick from the full PDR brand palette and textures. Prefer your own? Use any picture — from your Memories or your PC — or design one in Collages with your logo. You're separated from your background right on your PC, with nothing uploaded, and any words in a picture stay the right way around.

A second camera, with controls:

Add a second camera and show both at once, or either on its own; resize each bubble on a simple slider — now up to four times bigger than before — zoom in on yourself, and let it pop in and out. Plug in another webcam and PDR picks it up automatically.

Cam only — go full-screen on camera:

Mid-recording, drop the desktop away and fill the screen with just your camera: one camera fills the screen, or show both side by side. Turn either camera on or off without leaving the mode — it never falls to a blank screen — then bring the desktop back whenever you like. The camera glides in and out with a smooth grow-and-shrink each way. Perfect for a talking-head intro before a screen walk-through, or an on-camera aside partway through.

Smaller recordings, and a save check:

A new Tiny quality makes much smaller files when size matters more than sharpness. And when you stop a recording, PDR asks whether to Save, Discard or keep going — so a mis-click can't lose a take; choosing Discard now leaves the recorder ready so you can go straight into another take.

Faster and smoother:

The Collages and Viewer windows open and can be moved the instant they appear — no more waiting for every thumbnail to load first — and zooming a carousel is smooth. The main window stays grabbable too: the background library scan that keeps Search & Discovery up to date no longer ties up the window, so you can move it the moment you reach for the titlebar.

Polish throughout:

Transparent images preview on a checkerboard instead of black, glow and shadow follow a cut-out's real edges rather than its box, and one consistent name ("Transparency") for see-through everywhere. Refreshing a library to make it searchable is sturdier and smarter — one clear progress message that can't be started twice, it saves as it goes so an interruption just picks up where it left off, and it no longer asks you to re-index photos already searchable from another drive. Plus tidier tooltips and dozens of smaller fixes.
