Best Practices: Reports (Your Audit Trail, Safety Net, and Secret Weapon)

PDR’s reports aren’t just “logs” — they are proof, protection, and power.

They exist so you can:

trust what just happened,

understand why it happened,

and come back months (or years) later knowing exactly how your library was transformed.

Most apps stop at “done”.
PDR gives you evidence.

1) What a Fix Report Actually Is

Every time you run Run Fix, PDR creates a Fix Report.

That report is a permanent snapshot of:

what was processed,

how dates were determined,

what was renamed,

what was excluded,

and what confidence level each decision had.

Think of it as a receipt for your memories.

2) Report Summary: Your High-Level Story

When a fix completes, you’ll see the Report Summary.

This answers the three most important questions immediately:

✔️ What happened?

Total files processed

How many were Confirmed, Recovered, or Marked

How many exact duplicates were removed from output

✔️ How trustworthy was the result?

Confirmed = strongest confidence

Recovered = intelligent reconstruction

Marked = fallback used (review recommended)

✔️ Is this a preview or the full truth?

The on-screen table is a preview

The full report is always saved and exportable

This lets PDR scale cleanly from hundreds to hundreds of thousands of files without overwhelming you.

3) The File Table: Where the Magic Becomes Concrete

The table inside the report shows a sample of individual file transformations:

Original Filename → New Filename

Confidence method used for each file

This is where many users have their “oh wow” moment.

Why?

Because you can now see things like:

A meaningless name like IMG_2040115_143022.jpg

Cleanly restored to 2024-01-15_14-30-22.jpg

Suddenly:

photos are sortable,

timelines make sense,

and chaos becomes history.

4) One of the Most Powerful (and Unexpected) Uses of Reports
🔍 “What happened to that photo?”

Reports are incredibly valuable after the fix.

Common real-world use cases:

You remember a photo by its old filename
→ the report tells you exactly what it became.

You want to locate a specific video that was renamed years ago
→ search the exported CSV or TXT.

You’re comparing a restored library against an older backup
→ the report gives you a clean mapping.

You need reassurance nothing was invented or guessed
→ the confidence column proves the method used.

For people dealing with family memories, legal records, work archives, or investigations, this is gold.

5) Duplicates: Quietly Handled, Clearly Reported

PDR removes exact duplicates from the output using cryptographic file hashes.

Important points:

One original is always preserved

Duplicates are excluded from output, not deleted from source

The report tells you exactly how many were excluded

This gives you:

a cleaner restored library,

without silently discarding anything,

and with full transparency if you ever need to explain what happened.

6) Reports History: Your Long-Term Memory

The Reports History screen is where PDR becomes truly defensible.

Each report entry shows:

Run date & time

Number of sources

Total files processed

Breakdown of Confirmed / Recovered / Marked

Destination path used

From here you can:

reopen any report,

export it again,

or compare runs over time.

Even if your Workspace changes later, reports remain valid.

7) Exporting Reports (CSV & TXT)

You can export reports in two formats:

📄 CSV (Spreadsheet-friendly)

Best for:

Excel / Google Sheets

Sorting, filtering, searching

Comparing against other datasets

Professional or investigative use

📄 TXT (Human-readable)

Best for:

Archiving

Sharing with non-technical users

Attaching to support tickets

Long-term personal records

Exports are generated from saved report data, not your current workspace — so they always reflect what actually happened at the time.

8) Why Reports Are a Must-Have (Not a Nice-to-Have)

Most tools rename files and leave you with:

no record,

no justification,

no way back.

PDR reports give you:

trust — you can see why each decision was made

safety — nothing is hidden or irreversible

control — you can audit, export, and revisit anytime

confidence — especially important when memories matter

They turn PDR from a utility into a system you can rely on.

9) Getting the Most Out of Reports (Best Practices)

Always keep your reports — even if the fix “looks right”

Export CSV if you think you’ll ever need to search or cross-reference

Export TXT if you want a permanent, readable record

Use Reports History instead of rerunning fixes just to “check something”

Treat reports as part of your archive, not temporary output

10) The Big Picture

PDR doesn’t just fix filenames.

It:

restores timelines,

preserves provenance,

and gives you documentation to back it all up.

That’s why the reports matter.

They’re not overhead —
they’re the proof that your memories were handled properly.
